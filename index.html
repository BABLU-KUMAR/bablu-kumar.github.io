About Me

Currently, I am in the final phase of my PhD in Computer Science and serve as a Senior Research Fellow (SRF). During my doctoral studies, I have developed advanced models for proactive autoscaling and resource allocation in cloud–edge environments using Deep Learning and Federated Learning frameworks. I have extensive experience in Python programming, along with a strong understanding of containerization and orchestration technologies such as Docker and Kubernetes. My research focuses on designing intelligent pod-scaling strategies, dynamic deployment mechanisms, efficient resource management techniques, secure aggregation protocols, and differential privacy approaches for training large language models on hybrid datasets. By analyzing complex datasets and developing predictive models, I aim to build scalable, resilient, and efficient systems capable of adapting to dynamic workloads. My specialization is in Cloud and Edge Computing, with a particular focus on the real-world application of Deep Learning and Federated Learning, supported by autoscaling approaches based on the MAPE-K (Monitor–Analyze–Plan–Execute over a Knowledge Base) framework.
